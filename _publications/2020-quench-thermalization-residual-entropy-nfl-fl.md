In this work, we study non-equilibrium dynamics across a transition from a Sachdev-Ye-Kitaev non-Fermi liquid to a Fermi liquid. We analyze sudden and slow quenches and characterize the resulting thermalization behavior.

We find distinct relaxation regimes across the non-Fermi-liquid and Fermi-liquid phases, including signatures associated with the residual entropy of the SYK sector. This work connects quench dynamics, thermalization, and non-Fermi-liquid physics in strongly interacting quantum systems.
