In this work, we study information scrambling and butterfly velocity in one-dimensional quantum spin glass chains. We analyze lattice generalizations of quantum spin glass models and compute chaos diagnostics such as the quantum Lyapunov exponent and butterfly velocity.

We investigate how scrambling behaves across paramagnetic and spin-glass regimes, and how glass transitions, quantum fluctuations, and thermal fluctuations influence nonlocal information spreading. This work connects quantum chaos, spin glass physics, and many-body scrambling in spatially extended systems.
