In this work, we study a generalization of Yukawa-SYK-type models in which Majorana fermions are coupled to disordered bosonic degrees of freedom described by a quantum \(p\)-spin glass model.

We analyze how the rugged glassy landscape modifies the fermionic Green's functions and the low-energy behavior of the SYK sector. In the quantum spin-glass phase, the coupling to glassy bosonic modes enhances the stability of the spin-glass phase and slows down imaginary-time dynamics. In the quantum paramagnetic phase, the coupling strongly modifies the fermionic dynamics and can wash out critical SYK behavior.

This work connects Sachdev-Ye-Kitaev physics, quantum glassiness, and coupled fermion-boson many-body dynamics.
