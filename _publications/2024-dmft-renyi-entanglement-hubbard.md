In this work, we develop a dynamical mean-field theory framework to compute Rényi entanglement entropy and mutual information in the Hubbard model. The approach uses a path-integral formulation for fermionic entanglement and maps the calculation to an effective impurity problem within DMFT.

We apply this method to study entanglement across metallic and insulating regimes of the Hubbard model, including the crossover from thermal entropy to entanglement entropy and the behavior of mutual information near the Mott metal-insulator transition.
