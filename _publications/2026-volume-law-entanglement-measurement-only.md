In this work, we study entanglement generation in measurement-only quantum dynamics. We show that volume-law entangled states can be generated without intrinsic unitary dynamics, using local, nonrandom, yet noncommuting measurements.

We construct a one-dimensional fermionic model with a main chain and an auxiliary chain, where generalized measurements are implemented through local coupling to detector qubits. Our results show that long-time states with volume-law entanglement and mutual information can emerge purely from nonunitary measurement dynamics.

This work highlights the potential of measurement protocols for controlled entanglement generation and for studying nonunitary many-body dynamics.
