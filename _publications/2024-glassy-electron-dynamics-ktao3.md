In this work, we report glassy electron dynamics in the good-metal regime of electron-doped KTaO<sub>3</sub>. The study combines experiments, density functional theory, and phenomenological modeling to understand slow carrier relaxation and aging-like behavior in a quantum paraelectric material.

The results suggest that quantum fluctuation-stabilized soft polar modes can promote glassy electronic dynamics even in a well-delocalized metallic regime. This work connects quantum fluctuations, glassiness, and electronic relaxation in correlated quantum materials.
