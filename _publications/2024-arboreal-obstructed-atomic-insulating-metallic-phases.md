In this work, we study fermionic phases on arboreal arenas described by tree graphs. These systems do not tessellate a manifold but still support a notion of translational symmetry.

We identify obstructed atomic insulating phases characterized by distinct edge states, entanglement patterns, and winding characteristics. We also show that these insulating phases are separated by metallic regions, where different metallic phases can be distinguished by winding properties and correlation functions.

This work connects topology, entanglement, and metallic behavior in non-Euclidean lattice geometries.
