My research lies at the interface of theoretical condensed matter physics, quantum information, and non-equilibrium quantum many-body dynamics. I am broadly interested in how complex quantum systems thermalize, scramble information, generate entanglement, and respond to measurements, dissipation, and non-Hermitian dynamics.

## Quantum chaos and information scrambling

I study how quantum information spreads in interacting many-body systems using diagnostics such as out-of-time-ordered correlators, quantum Lyapunov exponents, and butterfly velocities. A central theme of my work is understanding how glassiness, disorder, and quantum fluctuations modify many-body chaos.

## Entanglement in many-body systems

I am interested in entanglement as a diagnostic of correlated quantum matter, including Fermi liquids, non-Fermi liquids, Hubbard models, and Sachdev-Ye-Kitaev-type systems. My work includes path-integral and dynamical mean-field approaches to Rényi entanglement entropy and mutual information.

## Measurements and non-unitary dynamics

I study how quantum measurements generate, suppress, or reorganize entanglement in many-body systems. This includes measurement-induced phase transitions, measurement-only dynamics, and the role of nonunitary evolution in producing highly entangled steady states.

## Open quantum systems and non-Hermitian physics

I work on dissipative and non-Hermitian many-body systems, including Lindblad dynamics and effective non-Hermitian descriptions. I am interested in how dissipation, non-reciprocity, exceptional points, and non-Hermitian dynamics affect correlations, spectra, and steady states.

## SYK models and quantum glassy systems

A recurring theme in my research is the use of solvable or controlled models such as Sachdev-Ye-Kitaev models and quantum p-spin glass models. These models provide a useful framework to study chaos, entanglement, glassiness, non-Fermi-liquid behavior, and quantum information diagnostics.

## Quantum resource theory and non-stabilizerness

I also study quantum resource-theoretic measures such as non-stabilizerness, or magic, in strongly interacting many-body systems. This direction aims to understand how quantum many-body dynamics generates resources beyond entanglement.
