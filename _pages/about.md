I am a CNRS postdoctoral researcher at Collège de France, working in theoretical condensed matter physics, quantum information, and non-equilibrium many-body systems.

My research focuses on quantum chaos, entanglement dynamics, open quantum systems, non-Hermitian physics, measurement-induced dynamics, Sachdev-Ye-Kitaev models, quantum p-spin glass models, and quantum resource theory.

Before joining Collège de France, I completed my PhD in Physics at the Indian Institute of Science, Bengaluru. My doctoral work focused on chaos, entanglement, and non-equilibrium dynamics in Hermitian and non-Hermitian quantum many-body systems.

Broadly, I am interested in understanding how complex quantum systems thermalize, scramble information, generate entanglement, and respond to measurements, dissipation, and non-Hermitian dynamics.

## Research interests

- Quantum chaos and information scrambling
- Entanglement and non-equilibrium quantum dynamics
- Open quantum systems and Lindblad dynamics
- Non-Hermitian many-body physics
- Measurement-induced phase transitions
- Quantum resource theory and non-stabilizerness
